The Problem

Single-Use Packaging Is a Massive Liability

Moving today still relies on a broken system—buy, use once, throw away. With over 13 million U.S. households relocating each year, hundreds of millions of cardboard boxes end up as waste.

Cardboard dominates logistics, supporting 90% of shipped goods in a $34B industry—but it comes at a cost: deforestation, high emissions, and unnecessary resource consumption.

This isn't just inefficient—it's unsustainable.
